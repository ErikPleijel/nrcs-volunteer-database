When you click View, the database opens everything it knows about that one person, gathered on a single page. <break time="0.6s" /> Near the top, the details are arranged in clear sections. <break time="0.7s" /> The Identity section comes first — name, photo, DB-number, gender, age, and signature. The things that tell you exactly who this person is. <break time="0.6s" /> Below it, the Contact section holds their email, their phone numbers, and their home address. <break time="0.6s" /> Then the Professional section: their occupation, their education, and where they work. <break time="0.7s" /> On the other side, the Red Cross Affiliation section shows where they belong in the Society — their branch, their division, their Red Cross unit, and any task force, along with whether they are a member or a volunteer. <break time="0.7s" /> The Activity Status section tells you how active they are — their lifecycle status, when they were last active, and whether they have donations, training, or first aid to their name. <break time="0.7s" /> And the System and Registration section records the practical details — how and when they registered, their email and password status, and when they last logged in. <break time="0.6s" /> Together, these sections give you a complete picture of the person.
