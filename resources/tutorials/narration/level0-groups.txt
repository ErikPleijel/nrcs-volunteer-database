There are three more terms to know — the groups people belong to. <break time="0.6s" /> First, a Red Cross unit. This is the permanent volunteer group within a division. It is the home base for volunteers — they belong to a unit, and they record their activity through it. <break time="0.7s" /> Second, a task force. A task force is a temporary group, created within a branch, but open to anyone in the database — volunteers and members alike. It might be an emergency response team, or simply a working group, like a database team. It lasts as long as you need it, and is dissolved when the work is done. <break time="0.7s" /> Third, an organisation. An organisation is a body outside the Red Cross — a company, or an institution. You use it to keep track of memberships and donations that come from that organisation, rather than from an individual person. <break time="0.6s" /> So: a unit is the permanent home for volunteers, a task force is a temporary group anyone can join, and an organisation is an outside body whose membership and giving you record.
