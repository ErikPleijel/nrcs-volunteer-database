Every user of this database has a role, and the role decides what you can see and do. <break time="0.7s" /> At national level, the national database administrator oversees all data operations, and authorizes roles across the country. <break time="0.4s" /> A national database assistant supports them with data entry and corrections. <break time="0.4s" /> A national observer can view reports and trends, but cannot change anything. <break time="0.7s" /> At branch level, the branch secretary and the branch database administrator share the same authority. They manage branch records, and authorize the assistants below them. <break time="0.4s" /> The branch database assistant supports them with data entry. <break time="0.7s" /> At division level, there are two kinds of assistant. <break time="0.4s" /> The finance assistant records everything, including membership payments and donations. <break time="0.4s" /> The operations assistant records volunteering and trainings, but does not handle money. <break time="0.7s" /> Don't worry about memorising all of this. You only need to know your own role — the rest will make sense as you use the system.
