Every record you create carries two references. <break time="0.6s" /> The first is the database reference. The system creates it automatically, one for every record. It begins with a short code showing the record type — F E E for a payment, V O L for volunteering, T R N for training, D O N for a donation — followed by the branch code. <break time="0.7s" /> The second is your own reference. This one is optional. It is the number from your paper record, often something simple like hash one two three four, and you type it into the Reference field. <break time="0.7s" /> Here is the golden rule: always write the database reference onto your paper record. <break time="0.7s" /> That way, the paper and the database always point to each other — and if you ever need to check one against the other, you can.
