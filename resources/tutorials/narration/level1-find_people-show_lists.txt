As you scroll down the person's record, you come to a series of lists — the full history of everything they have done with the Red Cross. <break time="0.7s" /> First, the Membership list. It shows their current membership status, and every payment they have made over the years. <break time="0.6s" /> Next, the Volunteering list — the activities they have taken part in, and the hours they have given. <break time="0.6s" /> Then the Training list, showing the courses they have completed. <break time="0.6s" /> After that, the Donations list — every gift they have made, whether in cash or in kind. <break time="0.6s" /> The Messages Sent list shows the campaigns and reminders this person has received. <break time="0.7s" /> And at the very bottom are two more lists: Printed Certificates, showing the certificates that have been issued to them, and Printed ID Cards, showing the cards that have been printed, along with their expiry dates. <break time="0.7s" /> So a single record holds the person's whole story — who they are, and everything they have done.
